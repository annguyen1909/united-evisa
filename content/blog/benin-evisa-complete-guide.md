## Complete Benin eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

**5. Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate**
- Mandatory for entry into Benin
- Must be valid and obtained at least 10 days before travel

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English or French (translations may be required for other languages)

## Travel Tips for Benin

### Best Time to Visit

**November to March (Dry Season - Peak Season)**
- Pleasant weather with minimal rainfall
- Ideal for wildlife viewing and outdoor activities
- Best time for Pendjari National Park
- Peak tourist season

**April to October (Wet Season)**
- Heavy rains, especially June-September
- Lush green landscapes
- Lower prices and fewer tourists
- Some roads may be impassable

### Popular Destinations

**Cultural Sites**
- Ouidah (UNESCO-listed city, historic slave route)
- Abomey (royal palaces, UNESCO World Heritage)
- Porto-Novo (capital with museums)
- Voodoo culture and festivals

**Wildlife**
- Pendjari National Park (premier wildlife destination)
- W National Park (shared with Niger and Burkina Faso)
- Wildlife viewing and safaris

**Unique Attractions**
- Ganvié (stilt village on Lake Nokoué)
- Cotonou (largest city with markets)
- Traditional markets and crafts

### Cultural Considerations

**Language**: French is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas and major cities.

**Currency**: West African CFA Franc (XOF). Credit cards accepted in major cities, cash preferred in rural areas.

**Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers based on service.

**Dress Code**: Casual dress is acceptable. Dress appropriately for weather and activities. Modest dress when visiting religious sites.

**Etiquette**: 
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions
- Learn basic French greetings (appreciated by locals)
- Respect voodoo culture and traditions

### Safety Tips

- Benin is generally safe for tourists
- Exercise standard precautions, especially in cities
- Use reputable tour operators for safaris
- Keep valuables secure
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

### Health and Vaccination Requirements

- **Yellow Fever**: Mandatory vaccination certificate required
- Routine vaccinations recommended
- Hepatitis A and B vaccinations recommended
- Tetanus and typhoid vaccinations recommended
- Malaria precautions essential (prophylaxis recommended)
- Drink bottled water only
- Comprehensive travel insurance highly recommended

### Beninese Cuisine

Benin offers diverse cuisine:
- **Pâte**: Corn or cassava porridge (staple food)
- **Sauce Gombo**: Okra sauce with meat
- **Fresh Seafood**: Especially in coastal areas
- **Grilled Meat**: Popular street food
