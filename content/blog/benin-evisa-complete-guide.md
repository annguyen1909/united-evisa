# Benin eVisa Complete Guide: Requirements, Application, and Travel Tips

## Why Visit Benin?

Benin is a country of fascinating history, vibrant markets, and stunning national parks. From the UNESCO-listed city of Ouidah to Pendjari National Park, Benin offers unique adventures for every traveler. Whether you're interested in culture, wildlife, or West African heritage, Benin is a must-visit destination.

## Benin eVisa Overview

The Benin eVisa system allows eligible foreign nationals to apply for and obtain an electronic visa online before traveling. This convenient process eliminates the need to visit an embassy or consulate, making travel to Benin easier and more efficient.

## Who Needs a Benin eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Benin. The eVisa is available to travelers from eligible countries for tourism. Some West African citizens may be visa-exempt for short stays, so always check your eligibility before applying.

## Types of Benin eVisas

### Tourist eVisa
- **Single Entry (30 days):** For tourism, cultural tours, and short stays. Government fee: $85 USD.
- **Multiple Entries (30 days):** For extended stays and multiple visits. Government fee: $112 USD.
- **Multiple Entries (90 days):** For long-term stays and multiple visits. Government fee: $140 USD.

## Benin eVisa Requirements

To apply for a Benin eVisa, you will need:
- A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay
- A recent passport-size photo
- Proof of accommodation in Benin
- Return or onward flight tickets
- Sufficient funds for your stay

All documents should be clear, legible, and in English or French. Additional documents may be required depending on your travel purpose.

Benin eVisa holders can enter through Cotonou International Airport and designated land borders. Always carry your eVisa approval letter and passport when traveling.

## Travel Tips for Benin

- **Best Time to Visit:** The dry season (November–March) is ideal for sightseeing and wildlife viewing. The rainy season (April–October) can make some areas difficult to access.
- **Popular Destinations:** Cotonou, Ouidah, Abomey, Ganvié, Pendjari National Park.
- **Local Language:** French
- **Currency:** West African CFA Franc (XOF)
- **Safety:** Benin is generally safe for tourists. Exercise standard precautions and respect local customs.
- **Visa Extensions:** Extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the Immigration Department in Cotonou for more information.
