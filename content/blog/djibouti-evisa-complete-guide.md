## Djibouti eVisa Overview

The Djibouti eVisa system allows eligible foreign nationals to apply for and obtain an electronic visa online before traveling. This convenient process eliminates the need to visit an embassy or consulate, making travel to Djibouti easier and more efficient.

## Who Needs a Djibouti eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Djibouti. The eVisa is available to travelers from eligible countries for tourism, business, or transit purposes. Always check your eligibility before applying.

## Djibouti eVisa Requirements

To apply for a Djibouti eVisa, you will need:
- A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay
- A recent passport-size photo
- Proof of accommodation in Djibouti
- Return or onward flight tickets
- Sufficient funds for your stay
- **For business eVisa:** Invitation letter or supporting documents

All documents should be clear, legible, and in English, Arabic, or French. Additional documents may be required depending on your travel purpose.

## Travel Tips for Djibouti

- **Best Time to Visit:** November to March offers the most comfortable weather. Avoid the extremely hot summer months (June–August).
- **Popular Destinations:** Djibouti City, Lake Assal, Lac Abbé, Arta Beach, Day Forest National Park.
- **Local Language:** Arabic and French
- **Currency:** Djiboutian Franc (DJF)
- **Safety:** Djibouti is generally safe for tourists. Exercise standard precautions and respect local customs.
- **Visa Extensions:** Extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the local immigration office for more information.
