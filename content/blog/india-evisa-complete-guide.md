## Complete India eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least two blank pages for entry stamps
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels
- Square format (equal width and height)

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

### Additional Requirements by Visa Type

**Business eVisa**
- Business invitation letter from an Indian company
- Company registration documents
- Letter from employer stating purpose of visit
- Proof of business meetings or conferences
- Trade license or professional credentials (if applicable)

**Medical eVisa**
- Medical letter from a recognized Indian hospital
- Treatment plan or medical consultation details
- Proof of medical condition (if applicable)
- Financial proof for medical expenses

**Ayush eVisa**
- Letter from recognized Ayurveda/Yoga center
- Treatment plan or program details
- Proof of enrollment in wellness program

**Conference eVisa**
- Conference invitation letter
- Conference registration confirmation
- Event details and schedule

**Student eVisa**
- Admission letter from Indian educational institution
- Proof of enrollment
- Financial proof for tuition and living expenses
- Academic transcripts (if required)

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English (translations may be required for non-English documents)

## Frequently Asked Questions About India eVisa

### Application Process

**Q: How long does it take to process an India eVisa?**
A: Normal processing takes 3 working days. Urgent processing is available for 1 working day approval.

**Q: Can I apply for an India eVisa if I have a criminal record?**
A: Having a criminal record doesn't automatically disqualify you, but you must declare it honestly in your application. Each case is assessed individually.

**Q: What if I make a mistake on my application?**
A: Contact our support team immediately. Depending on the processing stage, corrections may be possible. It's crucial to ensure accuracy before submission.

**Q: Do children need separate India eVisas?**
A: Yes, each traveler regardless of age needs their own eVisa. Minors require additional documentation.

### Travel and Entry

**Q: Can I extend my India visa once in the country?**
A: Yes, but only through the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) for valid reasons such as medical treatment, business, or academic purposes.

**Q: Can I use my India eVisa for multiple entries?**
A: It depends on your visa type. Tourist eVisas offer double entry (30 days) or multiple entry (1 year or 5 years). Business eVisas allow multiple entries.

**Q: Which airports can I use to enter India with an eVisa?**
A: India eVisa holders can enter through 28 designated international airports. Land border crossings require a regular visa.

**Q: What happens if my India eVisa is denied?**
A: Denials are rare, but if it occurs, you'll receive notification with the reason. Our team can help you address the issues and resubmit if appropriate.

### Requirements and Documents

**Q: What documents do I need for an India eVisa?**
A: You need a valid passport, recent passport photo, proof of accommodation, return flight tickets, and sufficient funds. Business, medical, and other visa types require additional documentation.

**Q: How long must my passport be valid for India?**
A: Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay in India.

**Q: Do I need travel insurance for India?**
A: While not mandatory, comprehensive travel insurance is highly recommended for medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and other unforeseen circumstances.
