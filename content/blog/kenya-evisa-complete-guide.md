# Kenya eVisa Complete Guide: Requirements, Application, and Travel Tips

## Why Visit Kenya?

Kenya is the heart of African safari adventures, home to incredible wildlife, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. From the Maasai Mara to the beaches of Mombasa, Kenya offers unforgettable experiences for every traveler. Whether you're planning a safari, beach holiday, or business trip, Kenya is a must-visit destination in East Africa.

## Kenya eVisa Overview

The Kenya eVisa system (Electronic Travel Authorization - ETA) allows eligible foreign nationals to apply for and obtain an electronic visa online before traveling. This convenient process eliminates the need to visit an embassy or consulate, making travel to Kenya easier and more efficient.

## Who Needs a Kenya eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Kenya. The eVisa is available to travelers from eligible countries for tourism, business, or transit purposes. Some nationalities may be visa-exempt for short stays, so always check your eligibility before applying.

## Types of Kenya eVisas

### Tourist ETA
- **Single Entry (90 days):** For safari tours, beach holidays, and cultural visits. Government fee: $95 USD.

### Business ETA
- **Single Entry (90 days):** For business meetings, conferences, and trade shows. Government fee: $95 USD.

### Transit ETA
- **Single Entry (72 hours):** For airport transit and short layovers. Government fee: $65 USD.

## Kenya eVisa Requirements

To apply for a Kenya eVisa, you will need:
- A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay
- A recent passport-size photo
- Proof of accommodation in Kenya
- Return or onward flight tickets
- Sufficient funds for your stay
- **For business eVisa:** Invitation letter or supporting documents

All documents should be clear, legible, and in English. Additional documents may be required depending on your travel purpose.

## Entry Points for Kenya eVisa

Kenya eVisa holders can enter through Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (Nairobi), Moi International Airport (Mombasa), and designated land borders. Always carry your eVisa approval letter and passport when traveling.

## Travel Tips for Kenya

- **Best Time to Visit:** Dry seasons (January–March and July–October) are ideal for safaris and outdoor activities.
- **Popular Destinations:** Nairobi, Maasai Mara, Amboseli National Park, Mombasa, Lake Nakuru.
- **Local Language:** English and Swahili
- **Currency:** Kenyan Shilling (KES)
- **Safety:** Kenya is generally safe for tourists, especially in tourist areas. Exercise standard precautions and use reputable tour operators for safaris.
- **Visa Extensions:** Extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the immigration office for more information.
