## Complete Laos eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English (translations may be required for non-English documents)

## Travel Tips for Laos

### Best Time to Visit

**November to March (Dry Season - Peak Season)**
- Pleasant weather with minimal rainfall
- Ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities
- Best time for temple visits and river cruises
- Peak tourist season

**April to May (Hot Season)**
- Very hot weather
- Lower prices and fewer tourists
- Early morning and evening activities recommended

**June to October (Wet Season)**
- Heavy rains, especially July-September
- Lush green landscapes
- Lower prices and fewer tourists
- Still possible to visit (rains are usually in afternoons)

### Popular Destinations

**Luang Prabang**
- UNESCO World Heritage city

**Vientiane**

### Cultural Considerations

**Language**: Lao is the official language. English is spoken in tourist areas, especially Luang Prabang and Vientiane.

**Currency**: Lao Kip (LAK). Thai Baht and US dollars are also accepted in tourist areas.

**Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers based on service.

**Dress Code**: Casual dress is acceptable. Dress modestly when visiting temples (cover shoulders and knees). Remove shoes when entering temples.

**Etiquette**: 
- Remove shoes when entering temples and homes
- Be respectful of religious sites and ceremonies
- Learn basic Lao greetings (appreciated by locals)
- Be respectful during morning alms giving ceremony

### Safety Tips

- Laos is very safe for tourists
- Exercise standard precautions
- Be cautious with street food (choose busy vendors)
- Keep valuables secure
- Use reputable tour operators
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

### Health and Vaccination Requirements

- No mandatory vaccinations for most travelers
- Routine vaccinations recommended
- Hepatitis A and B vaccinations recommended
- Tetanus and typhoid vaccinations recommended
- Malaria precautions recommended in some regions
- Japanese encephalitis vaccination recommended for rural areas
- Drink bottled water only
- Comprehensive travel insurance highly recommended
