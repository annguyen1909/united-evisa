## Complete Sri Lanka eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

### Additional Requirements for Business Travelers

- Business invitation letter from a Sri Lankan company
- Company registration documents
- Letter from employer stating purpose of visit
- Proof of business meetings or conferences

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English (translations may be required for non-English documents)

### Cultural Considerations

**Language**: Sinhala and Tamil are official languages. English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas.

**Currency**: Sri Lankan Rupee (LKR). Credit cards accepted in major cities, cash preferred in rural areas.

**Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers based on service.

**Dress Code**: Casual dress is acceptable. Dress modestly when visiting temples (cover shoulders and knees). Remove shoes when entering temples.

**Etiquette**: 
- Remove shoes when entering temples and homes
- Use right hand for giving/receiving items
- Be respectful of religious sites and ceremonies
- Learn basic Sinhala/Tamil phrases (appreciated by locals)

### Safety Tips

- Sri Lanka is generally safe for tourists
- Exercise standard precautions in cities
- Be cautious with street food (choose busy vendors)
- Keep valuables secure
- Use reputable tour operators
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

### Health and Vaccination Requirements

**Q: How long does it take to process a Sri Lanka eVisa?**

A: Having a criminal record doesn't automatically disqualify you, but you must declare it honestly in your application. Each case is assessed individually.

A: Contact our support team immediately. Depending on the processing stage, corrections may be possible. It's crucial to ensure accuracy before submission.

**Q: Do children need separate Sri Lanka eVisas?**
A: Yes, each traveler regardless of age needs their own eVisa. Minors require additional documentation.

### Travel and Entry

**Q: Can I extend my Sri Lanka visa once in the country?**
A: Yes, extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the Department of Immigration and Emigration for details and procedures.

**Q: Can I use my Sri Lanka eVisa for multiple entries?**
A: It depends on your visa type. Tourist eVisas allow double entry, business eVisas allow multiple entries.

**Q: Which airports can I use to enter Sri Lanka with an eVisa?**
A: Sri Lanka eVisa holders can enter through Bandaranaike International Airport (Colombo) and other designated international airports.

**Q: What happens if my Sri Lanka eVisa is denied?**
A: Denials are rare, but if it occurs, you'll receive notification with the reason. Our team can help you address the issues and resubmit if appropriate.

### Requirements and Documents

**Q: What documents do I need for a Sri Lanka eVisa?**
A: You need a valid passport, recent passport photo, proof of accommodation, return flight tickets, and sufficient funds. Business travelers may need additional documentation.

**Q: How long must my passport be valid for Sri Lanka?**
A: Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay in Sri Lanka.

**Q: Do I need travel insurance for Sri Lanka?**
A: While not mandatory, comprehensive travel insurance is highly recommended for medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and other unforeseen circumstances.
