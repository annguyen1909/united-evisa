## Complete Tanzania eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

**5. Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate**
- Mandatory for entry into Tanzania (if arriving from yellow fever risk countries)
- Must be valid and obtained at least 10 days before travel

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English (translations may be required for non-English documents)

## Travel Tips for Tanzania

### Best Time to Visit

**June to October (Dry Season - Peak Season)**
- Pleasant weather with minimal rainfall
- Ideal for wildlife viewing and safari activities
- Best time for Great Migration viewing
- Peak tourist season with higher prices

**December to February (Short Dry Season)**
- Warm weather
- Good for wildlife viewing
- Ideal for climbing Mount Kilimanjaro
- Peak season for Zanzibar beaches

**March to May (Long Wet Season)**
- Heavy rains, especially in April-May
- Lush green landscapes
- Lower prices and fewer tourists
- Some roads may be impassable

**November (Short Wet Season)**
- Variable weather
- Lower prices
- Still good for wildlife viewing

### Popular Destinations

**Northern Safari Circuit**
- Serengeti National Park (Great Migration)
- Ngorongoro Crater (incredible wildlife density)
- Tarangire National Park (elephant herds)
- Lake Manyara National Park (tree-climbing lions)
- Arusha (gateway city)

**Mount Kilimanjaro**
- Africa's highest peak (5,895 meters)
- Multiple climbing routes
- Requires proper preparation and guides
- Best climbed during dry seasons

**Zanzibar**
- Stone Town (UNESCO World Heritage site)
- Pristine beaches (Nungwi, Kendwa, Paje)
- Spice tours
- Diving and water sports
- Historic slave trade sites

**Southern Circuit**
- Selous Game Reserve (largest in Africa)
- Ruaha National Park
- Mikumi National Park
- Less crowded than northern circuit

### Cultural Considerations

**Language**: Swahili and English are official languages. English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

**Currency**: Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). US dollars are also accepted in tourist areas.

**Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers based on service.

**Dress Code**: Casual dress is acceptable. Dress appropriately for weather and activities. Modest dress when visiting local communities and Zanzibar.

**Etiquette**: 
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions
- Ask permission before taking photos of people
- Learn basic Swahili greetings (appreciated by locals)

### Safety Tips

- Tanzania is generally safe for tourists
- Exercise standard precautions in cities
- Use reputable tour operators for safaris and climbing
- Keep valuables secure
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

### Health and Vaccination Requirements

- **Yellow Fever**: Vaccination certificate required if arriving from risk countries
- Routine vaccinations recommended
