# Uganda eVisa Complete Guide: Requirements, Application, and Travel Tips

## Why Visit Uganda?

Uganda, known as the "Pearl of Africa," is famous for its incredible mountain gorilla experiences, diverse wildlife, and stunning landscapes. From gorilla trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest to the source of the Nile at Jinja, Uganda offers authentic African adventures. Whether you're interested in gorilla encounters, safari experiences, or cultural exploration, Uganda provides life-changing wildlife experiences.

## Uganda eVisa Overview

The Uganda eVisa system allows eligible foreign nationals to apply for and obtain an electronic visa online before traveling. This convenient process eliminates the need to visit an embassy or consulate, making travel to Uganda easier and more efficient.

## Who Needs a Uganda eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Uganda. The eVisa is available to travelers from eligible countries for tourism and transit purposes. Some East African countries may have visa exemptions, so always check your eligibility before applying.

## Types of Uganda eVisas

### East Africa eVisa
- **Multiple Entries (90 days):** For multi-country travel to Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda. Government fee: $150 USD.

### Transit eVisa
- **Single Entry (7 days):** For airport transit and quick connections. Government fee: $90 USD.

## Uganda eVisa Requirements

To apply for a Uganda eVisa, you will need:
- A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay
- A recent passport-size photo
- Proof of accommodation in Uganda
- Return or onward flight tickets
- Sufficient funds for your stay
- **For transit eVisa:** Proof of onward travel

All documents should be clear, legible, and in English. Additional documents may be required depending on your travel purpose.

## Entry Points for Uganda eVisa

Uganda eVisa holders can enter through Entebbe International Airport and designated land borders with Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, and Democratic Republic of Congo. Always carry your eVisa approval letter and passport when traveling.

## Travel Tips for Uganda

- **Best Time to Visit:** June to August and December to February offer the best weather for gorilla trekking and wildlife viewing.
- **Popular Destinations:** Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, Queen Elizabeth National Park, Murchison Falls, Jinja, Lake Bunyonyi.
- **Local Language:** English and Luganda
- **Currency:** Ugandan Shilling (UGX)
- **Safety:** Uganda is generally safe for tourists. Use reputable tour operators and follow local guidance.
- **Gorilla Trekking:** Book gorilla permits well in advance. The experience requires moderate fitness levels.
- **Health:** Yellow fever vaccination is required. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended.
- **Wildlife:** Uganda offers excellent opportunities to see mountain gorillas, chimpanzees, and diverse wildlife.
- **Visa Extensions:** Extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the immigration office for more information.
