## Complete Uganda eVisa Requirements

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

**1. Valid Passport**
- Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay
- Must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of the bio-data page required

**2. Passport-Size Photograph**
- Recent photo taken within the last 6 months
- White or light-colored background
- Clear facial features, no glasses or head coverings (except for religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**3. Travel Documentation**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended)

**4. Financial Proof**
- Bank statements showing sufficient funds for your stay
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks
- Proof of employment or financial support

**5. Yellow Fever Vaccination Certificate**
- Mandatory for entry into Uganda
- Must be valid and obtained at least 10 days before travel

### Document Quality Standards

All documents must meet these specifications:
- **File Format**: PDF or JPEG only
- **File Size**: Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality**: Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language**: English (translations may be required for non-English documents)

## Travel Tips for Uganda

### Best Time to Visit

**June to August and December to February (Dry Seasons)**
- Pleasant weather with less rainfall
- Ideal for gorilla trekking and safari activities
- Best wildlife viewing conditions
- Peak tourist season

**March to May and September to November (Wet Seasons)**
- Heavy rains, especially in April-May
- Lush green landscapes
- Lower prices and fewer tourists
- Still possible to visit (rains are usually in afternoons)

### Popular Destinations

**Gorilla Trekking**
- Bwindi Impenetrable Forest (most popular)
- Mgahinga Gorilla National Park
- Permits required (book well in advance)
- Life-changing wildlife experience

**National Parks**
- Murchison Falls National Park (Big Five)
- Queen Elizabeth National Park (tree-climbing lions)
- Kibale National Park (chimpanzee trekking)
- Kidepo Valley National Park (remote wilderness)

**Adventure Activities**
- Jinja (source of the Nile, white-water rafting)
- Lake Victoria (fishing and water sports)
- Rwenzori Mountains (hiking and climbing)

### Cultural Considerations

**Language**: English is the official language. Luganda and other local languages are also spoken.

**Currency**: Ugandan Shilling (UGX). US dollars are also accepted in tourist areas.

**Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated. 10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers based on service.

**Dress Code**: Casual dress is acceptable. Dress appropriately for weather and activities. Modest dress when visiting local communities.

**Etiquette**: 
- Be respectful of local customs and traditions
- Ask permission before taking photos of people
- Learn basic greetings (appreciated by locals)

### Safety Tips

- Uganda is generally safe for tourists
- Exercise standard precautions in cities
- Use reputable tour operators for gorilla trekking and safaris
- Keep valuables secure
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance

### Health and Vaccination Requirements

- **Yellow Fever**: Mandatory vaccination certificate required
- Routine vaccinations recommended
- Hepatitis A and B vaccinations recommended
- Tetanus and typhoid vaccinations recommended
- Malaria precautions essential (prophylaxis recommended)
- Drink bottled water only
- Comprehensive travel insurance highly recommended

### Ugandan Cuisine

Uganda offers diverse cuisine:
- **Matoke**: Steamed green bananas (staple food)
- **Rolex**: Chapati with eggs and vegetables
- **Fresh Fruits**: Pineapple, mango, passion fruit
- **Grilled Meat**: Especially goat and chicken
- **Groundnut Sauce**: Peanut sauce with various dishes
