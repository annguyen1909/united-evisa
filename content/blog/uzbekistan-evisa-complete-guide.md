## Uzbekistan eVisa Overview

The Uzbekistan eVisa system allows eligible foreign nationals to apply for and obtain an electronic visa online before traveling. This convenient process eliminates the need to visit an embassy or consulate, making travel to Uzbekistan easier and more efficient.

## Who Needs an Uzbekistan eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Uzbekistan. The eVisa is available to travelers from eligible countries for tourism purposes. Some CIS countries and neighboring states may have visa exemptions, so always check your eligibility before applying.

## Uzbekistan eVisa Requirements

To apply for an Uzbekistan eVisa, you will need:
- A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay
- A recent passport-size photo
- Proof of accommodation in Uzbekistan
- Return or onward flight tickets
- Sufficient funds for your stay
- Travel insurance (recommended)

All documents should be clear, legible, and in English. Additional documents may be required depending on your nationality.

## Travel Tips for Uzbekistan

- **Best Time to Visit:** April to June and September to November offer pleasant weather. Avoid summer due to extreme heat.
- **Popular Destinations:** Tashkent, Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva, Fergana Valley, Nukus.
- **Local Language:** Uzbek and Russian
- **Currency:** Uzbekistani Som (UZS)
- **Safety:** Uzbekistan is very safe for tourists with low crime rates and friendly locals.
- **Cultural Sites:** Uzbekistan has multiple UNESCO World Heritage Sites including historic city centers.
- **Architecture:** Marvel at stunning Islamic architecture including madrasas, mosques, and mausoleums.
- **Silk Road Heritage:** Follow ancient trade routes and explore historic caravanserais.
- **Visa Extensions:** Extensions are possible for valid reasons. Visit the local immigration office for more information.
