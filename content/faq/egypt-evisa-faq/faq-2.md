# Egypt eVisa Travel Tips and Cultural Guide: Complete Information

This comprehensive guide provides essential travel tips, cultural guidelines, and practical information for visiting Egypt with an eVisa, ensuring a safe, respectful, and enjoyable journey through this ancient land.

## Essential Travel Documents

### What documents should I carry in Egypt?

**Required Documents:**
- **Original Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months, with entry stamp
- **Printed eVisa**: Multiple copies recommended
- **Return Flight Tickets**: Proof of onward travel
- **Accommodation Proof**: Hotel bookings or invitation letters
- **Travel Insurance**: Highly recommended
- **Emergency Contacts**: Embassy information, local contacts

**Additional Recommendations:**
- Copies of all documents (stored separately)
- Digital copies on mobile device
- Passport photos (for extensions if needed)
- Credit cards and cash

## Health and Safety Information

### What health precautions should I take in Egypt?

**Water and Food Safety:**
- **Drink Only**: Bottled water (widely available)
- **Avoid**: Tap water, ice (unless from bottled water)
- **Food**: Generally safe in hotels and reputable restaurants
- **Street Food**: Choose busy vendors with high turnover
- **Fruits**: Eat only fruits you can peel yourself

**Medical Preparation:**
- Bring prescription medications with original labels
- Pack a basic first-aid kit
- Comprehensive travel insurance with medical coverage
- Know location of nearest hospital or clinic

**Common Health Issues:**
- Traveler's diarrhea (common, usually mild)
- Heat-related illnesses (stay hydrated, especially in summer)
- Sunburn (use sunscreen, cover up)
- Dehydration (drink plenty of water)

### Is Egypt safe for tourists?

Egypt is generally safe for tourists, especially in:

- Major tourist destinations (Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, Red Sea resorts)
- Tourist areas and organized tours
- Reputable hotels and accommodations
- Popular archaeological sites

**Safety Tips:**
- Exercise standard travel precautions
- Use reputable tour operators
- Keep valuables secure
- Stay informed about local conditions
- Follow advice from local authorities and tour guides
- Avoid isolated areas, especially at night
- Be cautious with traffic

**Women Travelers:**
- Generally safe, but dress modestly
- Avoid isolated areas, especially at night
- Use reputable transportation services
- Consider traveling with groups in some areas

## Cultural Guidelines and Etiquette

### What should I know about Egyptian customs and culture?

**Cultural Diversity:**
Egypt has a rich cultural heritage blending ancient Pharaonic, Islamic, Coptic, and modern influences.

**Dress Code:**
- **Modest Dress**: Cover shoulders and knees, especially at religious sites
- **Mosques**: Remove shoes, cover head (women), dress modestly
- **Tourist Areas**: More relaxed, but still respectful
- **Beaches**: Swimwear acceptable at resort beaches

**Religious Sites:**
- **Mosques**: Remove shoes, cover head (women), dress modestly
- **Churches**: Dress respectfully, remove hats
- **Temples**: Generally more relaxed, but still respectful
- **Photography**: Check if allowed, some sites prohibit flash

**Social Etiquette:**
- **Greetings**: "As-salamu alaykum" (peace be upon you) appreciated
- **Right Hand**: Use for eating, greeting, giving/receiving
- **Public Behavior**: Avoid public displays of affection
- **Bargaining**: Common in markets and bazaars (be polite)
- **Tipping**: Customary (10% in restaurants, tip guides and drivers)

**Language:**
- **Arabic**: Official language
- **English**: Widely spoken in tourist areas
- **French**: Also spoken in some areas
- **Learning Basics**: A few Arabic phrases appreciated

## Practical Travel Information

### What currency is used in Egypt?

**Currency Information:**
- **Egyptian Pound (EGP)**: Official currency
- **Exchange Rate**: Check current rates before travel
- **ATMs**: Widely available in cities and tourist areas
- **Credit Cards**: Accepted in hotels, restaurants, and major shops
- **Cash**: Essential for markets, taxis, and small vendors

**Money Management Tips:**
- Inform your bank about travel plans
- Bring backup cards
- Use ATMs in secure locations
- Keep small denominations for tips
- US dollars sometimes accepted in tourist areas

### What transportation options are available in Egypt?

**Domestic Transportation:**
- **Domestic Flights**: Quick connections between major cities
- **Trains**: Comfortable options for longer distances
- **Buses**: Comprehensive network, budget-friendly
- **Taxis**: Use reputable services or ride-sharing apps
- **Nile Cruises**: Popular way to see Luxor and Aswan

**Getting Around Cities:**
- **Taxis**: Negotiate fares or use meters
- **Uber/Careem**: Available in major cities
- **Metro**: Available in Cairo
- **Walking**: Good in tourist areas, be cautious with traffic

**Safety Tips:**
- Use reputable transportation services
- Prepaid taxi counters at airports are safe
- Be cautious with traffic (can be chaotic)
- Book tours through reputable operators

### What should I pack for Egypt?

**Essential Items:**
- Lightweight, modest clothing (cover shoulders and knees)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Scarf (for women, for mosque visits)
- Insect repellent
- Basic first-aid kit
- Prescription medications
- Copies of important documents

**Seasonal Considerations:**
- **Summer**: Light, breathable clothing, extra sun protection
- **Winter**: Warmer clothing for evenings, especially in desert
- **Year-round**: Modest dress for religious sites

## Top Destinations and Experiences

### What are the must-see destinations in Egypt?

**Historical Sites:**
- **Pyramids of Giza**: One of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
- **Sphinx**: Iconic monument near the pyramids
- **Valley of the Kings**: Ancient pharaonic tombs
- **Karnak Temple**: Massive temple complex in Luxor
- **Abu Simbel**: Magnificent temples relocated from Aswan
- **Philae Temple**: Beautiful island temple

**Cities:**
- **Cairo**: Capital with museums, markets, and culture
- **Luxor**: "World's greatest open-air museum"
- **Aswan**: Nubian culture and beautiful Nile setting
- **Alexandria**: Mediterranean city with historic sites

**Red Sea:**
- **Sharm El Sheikh**: World-class diving and beaches
- **Hurghada**: Beach resort with water activities
- **Dahab**: Relaxed diving destination

### What are the best experiences in Egypt?

**Cultural Experiences:**
- **Nile Cruise**: Luxor to Aswan or vice versa
- **Felucca Sailing**: Traditional sailboat on the Nile
- **Desert Tours**: Sahara adventures and Bedouin experiences
- **Camel Rides**: At the pyramids and other sites
- **Sound and Light Shows**: At major historical sites

**Adventure Activities:**
- **Diving**: World-class Red Sea diving
- **Snorkeling**: Beautiful coral reefs
- **Desert Safaris**: Quad biking, sandboarding
- **Hot Air Ballooning**: Over Luxor and the Valley of the Kings

**Cultural Immersion:**
- **Nubian Villages**: Experience local culture in Aswan
- **Traditional Markets**: Khan el-Khalili in Cairo
- **Museums**: Egyptian Museum, Nubian Museum
- **Traditional Food**: Try local Egyptian cuisine

## Food and Dining

### What should I know about Egyptian cuisine?

**Popular Dishes:**
- **Koshari**: National dish with rice, lentils, pasta
- **Ful Medames**: Fava beans, traditional breakfast
- **Ta'ameya**: Egyptian falafel
- **Shawarma**: Grilled meat wraps
- **Fresh Seafood**: Especially in coastal areas

**Dining Tips:**
- **Restaurants**: Generally safe, choose reputable places
- **Street Food**: Can be delicious, choose busy vendors
- **Water**: Always drink bottled water
- **Tipping**: 10% customary in restaurants
- **Vegetarian Options**: Many available

## Shopping and Souvenirs

### What should I know about shopping in Egypt?

**Popular Souvenirs:**
- **Papyrus**: Ancient Egyptian paper art
- **Alabaster**: Carved items and vases
- **Perfumes**: Egyptian essences and oils
- **Spices**: From local markets
- **Jewelry**: Gold and silver items
- **Carpets**: Handwoven rugs

**Shopping Tips:**
- **Bargaining**: Expected in markets and bazaars
- **Fixed Prices**: In government shops and some stores
- **Quality**: Check quality before purchasing
- **Receipts**: Keep for customs if needed
- **Authenticity**: Be cautious of fake antiquities
