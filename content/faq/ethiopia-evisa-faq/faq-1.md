## Required Documents for Ethiopia eVisa

### Essential Documents for All Applicants

To successfully apply for an Ethiopia eVisa, you'll need:

**Passport Requirements:**
- Valid passport with at least 6 months remaining beyond your intended stay
- Passport must have at least one blank page for entry stamp
- Clear, high-quality scan of passport bio-data page

**Photograph Requirements:**
- Recent passport-size photo (taken within last 6 months)
- White background, clear facial features
- No glasses, hats, or head coverings (except religious purposes)
- JPEG format, minimum 600x600 pixels

**Travel Documentation:**
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or address)
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Travel itinerary (recommended but not always mandatory)

**Financial Requirements:**
- Proof of sufficient funds for your stay
- Bank statements (last 3 months recommended)
- Credit card statements or traveler's checks

### Document Quality and Format

All documents must meet specific requirements:
- **File format:** PDF or JPEG only
- **File size:** Maximum 5MB per document
- **Quality:** Clear, legible, and in color
- **Language:** Documents in English (translations may be required)

## Ethiopia Travel Information and Tips

### Best Time to Visit Ethiopia

**Seasonal Considerations**

**Dry Season (October - May)**
- Pleasant temperatures and clear skies
- Ideal for sightseeing, trekking, and festivals
- Peak travel season for most regions

**Rainy Season (June - September)**
- Lush green landscapes
- Some areas may be less accessible
- Fewer tourists and lower prices

### Popular Destinations in Ethiopia

**Addis Ababa - The Capital City**
- National Museum of Ethiopia (home of Lucy)
- Holy Trinity Cathedral
- Vibrant markets and coffee culture
- Modern art and nightlife

**Lalibela**
- UNESCO World Heritage site
- Famous rock-hewn churches
- Pilgrimage destination
- Unique religious festivals

**Simien Mountains National Park**
- Dramatic mountain scenery
- Trekking and wildlife (gelada baboons, ibex)
- UNESCO World Heritage site
- Adventure and photography

**Lake Tana & Bahir Dar**
- Source of the Blue Nile
- Monasteries on lake islands
- Scenic boat trips
- Waterfalls and birdwatching

### Cultural Considerations

**Language and Communication**
- Official language: Amharic
- English widely spoken in tourist areas
- Learn basic Amharic phrases for a better experience

**Currency and Payments**
- Ethiopian Birr (ETB) is the official currency
- Credit cards accepted in major cities
- Cash preferred in rural areas
- ATMs available in urban centers

**Local Customs and Etiquette**
- Ethiopians are known for hospitality
- Respect religious sites and traditions
- Dress modestly, especially in churches
- Tipping 10% is customary in restaurants

### Safety and Health Information

**General Safety**
Ethiopia is considered safe for tourists, especially in major destinations:
- Low violent crime rates
- Safe for solo travelers and families
- Political stability in tourist regions
- Good healthcare in major cities

**Health Precautions**
- No special vaccinations required for most travelers
- Tap water is not recommended—drink bottled water
- Comprehensive travel insurance advised
- Altitude considerations for mountain regions

**Emergency Contacts**
- Police: 991
- Medical emergency: 907
- Fire department: 939
- Tourist police: available in Addis Ababa and major cities
