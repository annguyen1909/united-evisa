## Do I need a visa to visit India?

Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter India. The eVisa system allows travelers from over 170 eligible countries to apply online before their trip. Some neighboring countries may have different visa requirements or exemptions, so it's important to check your specific nationality's eligibility on our platform before applying.

## How long does it take to process an India eVisa?

Processing time for India eVisas typically takes 3 working days for normal processing and 1 working day for super urgent processing. We recommend applying at least 1 week before your intended travel date to avoid any delays. Our platform has a 98% approval rate, and we provide 24/7 support throughout the application process.

## What documents do I need for an India eVisa?

You'll need a valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay, a recent passport-size photo, proof of accommodation in India, return or onward flight tickets, and sufficient funds for your stay. For business visas, you'll also need an invitation letter from the Indian company. For medical visas, a medical letter from a recognized hospital is required. All documents should be clear, legible, and in English.

## Can I extend my India visa while in the country?

Yes, you can extend your India visa while in the country, but only through the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) for valid reasons such as medical treatment, business needs, or academic purposes. Extensions are not automatic and require proper documentation and justification. The process can take several days, so plan accordingly.

## What are the different types of India eVisas available?

India offers several eVisa types: Tourist eVisa (30 days double entry, 1 year multiple entry, 5 years multiple entry), Business eVisa (1 year multiple entry), Medical eVisa (60 days triple entry), Ayush eVisa for wellness treatments (60 days triple entry), Conference eVisa (30 days single entry), and Student eVisa (duration of course). Each serves specific travel purposes with different validity periods.

## How much does an India eVisa cost?

India eVisa fees vary by nationality and visa type. Tourist eVisas range from $30-$110 depending on duration and nationality. Business eVisas cost $30-$130. Medical, Ayush, Conference, and Student eVisas range from $30-$130. The exact fee for your nationality will be displayed during the application process on our platform.

## Which airports can I use to enter India with an eVisa?

India eVisa holders can enter through 28 designated airports including major ones like Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Bangalore (BLR), Chennai (MAA), Kolkata (CCU), Hyderabad (HYD), and others. You can also enter through 5 designated seaports: Mumbai, Chennai, Cochin, Goa, New Mangalore, and Port Blair. Land border crossings require a regular visa, not an eVisa.

## Is it safe to travel to India?

Yes, India is generally safe for tourists, especially in popular tourist areas and major cities. However, it's important to take standard safety precautions like avoiding isolated areas at night, keeping your belongings secure, and staying informed about local conditions. Most tourist destinations have good security measures in place, and millions of visitors travel to India safely each year.

## What is the best time to visit India?

The best time to visit India depends on the region. Generally, October to March is ideal for most parts of India due to cooler weather. The monsoon season (June-September) brings lush landscapes but can make some areas difficult to access. Northern India is best visited in winter, while southern India can be visited year-round. Hill stations are pleasant during summer months.

## Can I work in India with a tourist eVisa?

No, tourist eVisas do not permit employment or business activities in India. If you need to work, conduct business, or attend conferences, you must apply for the appropriate visa category such as Business eVisa, Conference eVisa, or a regular work visa depending on your specific needs and duration of stay.
