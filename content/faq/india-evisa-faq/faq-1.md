# India eVisa FAQ: Complete Guide to All Your Questions

Get comprehensive answers to all your India eVisa questions. This detailed FAQ covers requirements, application process, processing times, fees, travel tips, and everything you need to know about visiting India.

## General India eVisa Questions

### What is an India eVisa?

An India eVisa is an electronic travel authorization that allows eligible foreign nationals to enter India for tourism, business, medical treatment, or transit purposes. The eVisa system enables travelers to apply online without visiting an embassy or consulate, making travel to India more convenient and accessible.

The India eVisa is linked to your passport and must be printed and presented upon arrival at Indian entry points. It's available in multiple types with different validity periods and entry options.

### Who needs an India eVisa?

Most foreign nationals require an India eVisa to enter India. This includes citizens from:

- **North America**: United States, Canada, Mexico
- **Europe**: All EU countries, United Kingdom, Norway, Switzerland, Russia
- **Asia-Pacific**: China, Japan, Australia, New Zealand, South Korea, Singapore
- **Middle East**: UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and most Middle Eastern countries
- **Africa**: Most African countries
- **South America**: Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Colombia, and most South American countries

**Visa-Exempt Nationalities (Limited):**
- Citizens of Bhutan and Nepal (special arrangements)
- Some diplomatic passport holders

**Important**: Always verify your specific nationality's requirements using our eligibility checker before applying.

### How long is an India eVisa valid?

India eVisas have different validity periods depending on the visa type:

- **Tourist eVisa (30 days)**: Valid for 30 days from entry date, double entry
- **Tourist eVisa (1 year)**: Valid for 1 year from issue date, multiple entries, up to 90 days per stay
- **Tourist eVisa (5 years)**: Valid for 5 years from issue date, multiple entries, up to 90 days per stay
- **Business eVisa (1 year)**: Valid for 1 year from issue date, multiple entries, up to 180 days per stay
- **Medical eVisa**: Valid for 60 days from entry date, triple entry

The eVisa must be used within its validity period. Once you enter India, the immigration officer will determine the exact length of stay.

### What are the different types of India eVisas?

India offers several eVisa types:

**1. Tourist eVisa (30 Days)**
- Purpose: Tourism, sightseeing, cultural exploration
- Validity: 30 days from entry, double entry
- Government Fee: $25-100 USD (varies by nationality)
- Processing Time: 3-5 working days (normal), 1-2 working days (urgent)

**2. Tourist eVisa (1 Year)**
- Purpose: Extended tourism with multiple entries
- Validity: 1 year from issue date, multiple entries, up to 90 days per stay
- Government Fee: $40-200 USD (varies by nationality)
- Processing Time: 3-5 working days (normal), 1-2 working days (urgent)

**3. Tourist eVisa (5 Years)**
- Purpose: Long-term tourism with multiple entries
- Validity: 5 years from issue date, multiple entries, up to 90 days per stay
- Government Fee: $80-400 USD (varies by nationality)
- Processing Time: 3-5 working days (normal), 1-2 working days (urgent)

**4. Business eVisa (1 Year)**
- Purpose: Business meetings, conferences, trade exhibitions
- Validity: 1 year from issue date, multiple entries, up to 180 days per stay
- Government Fee: Varies by nationality
- Processing Time: 3-5 working days (normal), 1-2 working days (urgent)

**5. Medical eVisa**
- Purpose: Medical treatment in India
- Validity: 60 days from entry, triple entry
- Government Fee: Varies by nationality
- Processing Time: 3-5 working days (normal), 1-2 working days (urgent)

### Can I extend my stay in India beyond my eVisa validity?

Tourist eVisas can be extended in exceptional circumstances through the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO). However:

- Extensions are **not guaranteed**
- Require valid reasons (medical emergency, flight cancellation, etc.)
- Must be applied for before visa expiry
- Different policies apply to different visa types

**Best Practice**: Plan your trip within your visa validity to avoid complications.

## Requirements and Eligibility Questions

### What documents do I need to carry when traveling to India with an eVisa?

When traveling to India, you must carry:

- **Original Passport**: Valid for at least 6 months, with at least 2 blank pages
- **Printed eVisa Approval Letter**: Multiple copies recommended
- **Return Flight Tickets**: Proof of onward travel
- **Proof of Sufficient Funds**: Bank statements or credit cards
- **Accommodation Proof**: Hotel bookings or invitation letters
- **Yellow Fever Certificate**: If arriving from risk countries

Keep physical copies as backup and digital copies on your mobile device.

### Are there any health requirements for traveling to India?

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- **Yellow Fever**: Vaccination certificate required if arriving from infected areas

**Recommended Vaccinations:**
- Routine vaccinations (measles, mumps, rubella, etc.)
- Hepatitis A and B
- Tetanus and typhoid
- Japanese encephalitis (for some regions)
- Rabies (for extended stays or rural areas)

**Important**: Consult with a travel health specialist before your trip to India.

### What should I know about Indian customs and culture?

**Cultural Considerations:**
- **Dress Modestly**: Especially when visiting religious sites
- **Remove Shoes**: Before entering homes and temples
- **Right Hand**: Preferred for eating and greeting
- **Respect Traditions**: During festivals and religious observances
- **Photography**: Ask permission before photographing people
- **Tipping**: Customary in restaurants and for services (10%)

**Religious Sites:**
- Cover head, shoulders, and knees
- Remove shoes and leather items
- Follow specific rules for each religion
- Be respectful and quiet

### What items are prohibited from bringing into India?

**Prohibited Items:**
- Narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances
- Weapons and ammunition
- Satellite phones (without permission)
- Certain electronic items
- Beef products (in some states)
- Counterfeit currency

**Restricted Items (Require Declaration):**
- Gold and silver above certain limits
- Currency above $5,000 USD equivalent
- Professional equipment
- Medications (with prescriptions)

**Important**: Check the latest customs regulations before packing to avoid issues at immigration.

### How should I handle money and payments in India?

**Currency:**
- **Indian Rupee (INR)**: Local currency
- **ATMs**: Widely available in cities
- **Credit Cards**: Accepted in hotels and restaurants in major cities
- **Cash**: Essential for rural areas and markets
- **US Dollars/Euros**: Can be exchanged at authorized dealers

**Tips:**
- Inform your bank about travel plans to avoid card blocks
- Carry cash for rural areas
- Use ATMs in secure locations
- Keep receipts for currency exchange

### What transportation options are available in India?

**Domestic Transportation:**
- **Railways**: Extensive network, book in advance (especially peak season)
- **Domestic Flights**: Quick connections between major cities
- **Buses**: Comprehensive network, budget-friendly
- **Taxis**: Use reputable services or ride-sharing apps
- **Prepaid Taxis**: Reliable and safe at airports

**Tips:**
- Book train tickets well in advance
- Use reputable transportation services
- Prepaid taxi counters at airports are safe
- Consider domestic flights for long distances

### Is it safe to drink water and eat street food in India?

**Water Safety:**
- **Bottled Water**: Always drink bottled or filtered water
- **Avoid Ice**: Unless you're sure it's made from purified water
- **Brushing Teeth**: Use bottled water
- **Hot Beverages**: Generally safe (tea, coffee)

**Food Safety:**
- **Street Food**: Choose busy stalls with high turnover
- **Hot Food**: Eat freshly cooked, hot food
- **Raw Items**: Avoid raw salads or unpeeled fruits from street vendors
- **Restaurant Food**: Generally safe in hotels and reputable restaurants
- **Personal Hygiene**: Wash hands frequently, use hand sanitizer

### What should I do in case of emergency while in India?

**Emergency Contacts:**
- **Police**: 100
- **Fire**: 101
- **Ambulance**: 102
- **Tourist Helpline**: 1363

**Important Actions:**
- Contact your embassy or consulate immediately
- Keep emergency contacts handy
- Inform someone of your travel plans
- Keep copies of important documents separately
- Travel insurance is highly recommended

**Healthcare:**
- Major cities have good healthcare facilities
- Medical evacuation insurance advisable for remote areas
- Keep prescription medications with original labels
