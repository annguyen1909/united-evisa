# India eVisa Travel Requirements and Tips: Complete Guide

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about traveling to India with an eVisa, including required documents, health requirements, cultural guidelines, transportation, safety tips, and practical travel advice.

## Travel Documents and Entry Requirements

### What documents do I need to carry when traveling to India with an eVisa?

When traveling to India, you must carry these essential documents:

**Required Documents:**
- **Original Passport**: Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay, with at least 2 blank pages for entry stamps
- **Printed eVisa Approval Letter**: Print multiple copies (keep one with you, one in luggage, digital copy on phone)
- **Return or Onward Flight Tickets**: Proof of departure from India
- **Proof of Sufficient Funds**: Bank statements, credit cards, or traveler's checks
- **Accommodation Proof**: Hotel bookings or invitation letters
- **Yellow Fever Certificate**: If arriving from yellow fever risk countries

**Additional Recommendations:**
- Travel insurance documents
- Emergency contact information
- Copies of all important documents (stored separately)
- Digital copies on mobile device or cloud storage

**Important**: Keep physical copies as backup. Immigration officers may request to see these documents upon arrival.

### What happens at immigration when I arrive in India?

Upon arrival in India with your eVisa:

1. **Present Documents**: Show your printed eVisa, valid passport, and required documents
2. **Immigration Check**: Officer verifies your eVisa and passport details
3. **Biometric Verification**: Fingerprints and photo may be taken
4. **Entry Stamp**: Receive entry stamp in your passport
5. **Customs Declaration**: Complete customs form if required

**Processing Time**: Usually 15-30 minutes, but may take longer during peak hours.

**Tips for Smooth Entry:**
- Have all documents ready and organized
- Answer questions clearly and honestly
- Be patient and respectful
- Follow immigration officer's instructions

## Health Requirements and Vaccinations

### Are there any health requirements for traveling to India?

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- **Yellow Fever Vaccination**: Required if arriving from or transiting through yellow fever risk countries
- Certificate must be obtained at least 10 days before travel
- Valid for 10 years

**Highly Recommended Vaccinations:**
- **Routine Vaccinations**: Measles, mumps, rubella, diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis
- **Hepatitis A and B**: Essential for travel to India
- **Typhoid**: Recommended for most travelers
- **Japanese Encephalitis**: For extended stays or rural travel
- **Rabies**: For extended stays, rural areas, or animal interactions

**Important**: Consult with a travel health specialist at least 4-6 weeks before your trip to India.

### What health precautions should I take in India?

**Water and Food Safety:**
- **Drink Only**: Bottled water or filtered/boiled water
- **Avoid**: Tap water, ice (unless from purified water), raw foods
- **Food**: Eat hot, freshly cooked food; avoid street food initially
- **Fruits**: Eat only fruits you can peel yourself

**Medical Preparation:**
- Bring prescription medications with original labels
- Pack a basic first-aid kit
- Comprehensive travel insurance with medical coverage
- Know location of nearest hospital or clinic

**Common Health Issues:**
- Traveler's diarrhea (common, usually mild)
- Heat-related illnesses (stay hydrated)
- Air pollution (consider masks in major cities)
- Mosquito-borne diseases (use repellent, consider prophylaxis)

## Cultural Guidelines and Etiquette

### What should I know about Indian customs and culture?

**Cultural Diversity:**
India is incredibly diverse with multiple religions, languages, and traditions. Understanding basic cultural norms enhances your travel experience.

**Dress Code:**
- **Modest Dress**: Cover shoulders and knees, especially at religious sites
- **Temples**: Remove shoes, cover head if required
- **Conservative Areas**: More modest dress expected
- **Urban Areas**: More relaxed, but still respectful

**Religious Sites:**
- **Remove Shoes**: Before entering temples, mosques, and some homes
- **Cover Head**: At Sikh temples (Gurudwaras)
- **No Leather**: At Hindu and Jain temples
- **Respect**: Be quiet, follow rules, don't take photos if prohibited

**Social Etiquette:**
- **Right Hand**: Use for eating, greeting, and giving/receiving items
- **Left Hand**: Considered unclean (used for personal hygiene)
- **Greetings**: "Namaste" with folded hands is respectful
- **Public Behavior**: Avoid public displays of affection
- **Photography**: Ask permission before photographing people

**Festivals and Observances:**
- Respect religious observances and festivals
- Some areas may have restrictions during festivals
- Check festival dates as they may affect travel

### What are important cultural considerations for visitors?

**Language:**
- **Official Languages**: Hindi and English
- **English**: Widely spoken in tourist areas and cities
- **Local Languages**: 22 official languages, hundreds of dialects
- **Learning Basics**: A few words in local language appreciated

**Religion:**
- **Major Religions**: Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, Jainism
- **Respect**: All religions and practices
- **Temples**: Active places of worship, not just tourist attractions
- **Prayer Times**: Be aware of prayer times in Muslim areas

**Business Culture:**
- **Punctuality**: Important for business meetings
- **Business Cards**: Exchange with both hands
- **Relationships**: Building relationships is important
- **Hierarchy**: Respect for seniority and authority

## Practical Travel Information

### How should I handle money and payments in India?

**Currency Information:**
- **Indian Rupee (INR)**: Official currency
- **Exchange Rate**: Check current rates before travel
- **ATMs**: Widely available in cities, less common in rural areas
- **Credit Cards**: Accepted in hotels, restaurants, and major shops in cities
- **Cash**: Essential for markets, rural areas, and small vendors

**Money Management Tips:**
- **Inform Bank**: Notify your bank about travel plans to avoid card blocks
- **Multiple Cards**: Bring backup cards
- **Cash Withdrawal**: Use ATMs in secure locations (banks, hotels)
- **Currency Exchange**: Use authorized dealers, keep receipts
- **Small Denominations**: Keep small bills for tips and small purchases

**Payment Methods:**
- **Cards**: Visa, MasterCard widely accepted
- **Digital Payments**: UPI, mobile wallets growing in popularity
- **US Dollars**: Can be exchanged at authorized dealers and airports
- **Traveler's Checks**: Less common, but accepted

### What transportation options are available in India?

**Domestic Flights:**
- **Network**: Extensive domestic flight network
- **Airlines**: Multiple carriers including low-cost options
- **Booking**: Book in advance for better prices
- **Airports**: Well-connected major cities

**Railways:**
- **Network**: One of the world's largest railway networks
- **Classes**: Multiple classes from basic to luxury
- **Booking**: Book well in advance, especially during peak season
- **Scenic Routes**: Some routes offer beautiful scenery

**Road Transportation:**
- **Buses**: Comprehensive network, budget-friendly
- **Taxis**: Use reputable services or ride-sharing apps
- **Auto-rickshaws**: Common in cities, negotiate fares
- **Car Rental**: Available with or without driver

**Safety Tips:**
- Use prepaid taxi counters at airports
- Book through reputable companies
- Avoid unlicensed operators
- Be cautious with traffic (can be chaotic in cities)

### Is it safe to drink water and eat street food in India?

**Water Safety:**
- **Bottled Water**: Always drink bottled or filtered water
- **Brands**: Stick to reputable brands (Bisleri, Kinley, Aquafina)
- **Ice**: Avoid ice unless you're certain it's from purified water
- **Brushing Teeth**: Use bottled water
- **Hot Beverages**: Tea and coffee are generally safe (boiled water)

**Food Safety:**
- **Street Food**: Can be delicious but choose carefully
  - Pick busy stalls with high turnover
  - Watch food being prepared
  - Eat hot, freshly cooked food
- **Restaurant Food**: Generally safe in hotels and reputable restaurants
- **Raw Foods**: Avoid raw salads, unpeeled fruits from street vendors
- **Dairy**: Be cautious with unpasteurized dairy products
- **Personal Hygiene**: Wash hands frequently, use hand sanitizer

**Gradual Adaptation:**
- Start with hotel/restaurant food
- Gradually try street food if comfortable
- Listen to your body
- Stay hydrated

## Safety and Emergency Information

### What should I do in case of emergency while in India?

**Emergency Contacts:**
- **Police**: 100
- **Fire**: 101
- **Ambulance**: 102
- **Tourist Helpline**: 1363 (24/7)
- **Women's Helpline**: 1091

**Important Actions:**
1. **Contact Embassy**: Reach out to your country's embassy or consulate immediately
2. **Local Authorities**: Contact local police or emergency services
3. **Travel Insurance**: Contact your insurance provider
4. **Documentation**: Keep copies of important documents separately
5. **Emergency Contacts**: Keep emergency contact information handy

**Pre-Travel Preparation:**
- Register with your embassy
- Share itinerary with family/friends
- Keep embassy contact information
- Purchase comprehensive travel insurance
- Know location of nearest hospital

**Healthcare:**
- **Major Cities**: Good healthcare facilities available
- **Rural Areas**: Limited facilities, medical evacuation may be needed
- **Medical Insurance**: Highly recommended with evacuation coverage
- **Prescription Medications**: Bring with original labels and prescriptions

### Is India safe for tourists?

India is generally safe for tourists, especially in:

- Major tourist destinations
- Popular cities and tourist areas
- Reputable hotels and accommodations
- Organized tour groups

**Safety Tips:**
- **Exercise Standard Precautions**: As you would in any foreign country
- **Avoid Isolated Areas**: Especially at night
- **Keep Valuables Secure**: Use hotel safes, don't flash expensive items
- **Use Reputable Services**: Tour operators, transportation, accommodations
- **Stay Informed**: Check travel advisories and local conditions
- **Trust Your Instincts**: If something feels wrong, avoid it

**Common Scams to Avoid:**
- Overcharging taxi/auto-rickshaw drivers
- Fake tour operators
- Touts offering "special deals"
- Currency exchange scams
- Fake tickets or permits

**Women Travelers:**
- Generally safe, but exercise extra caution
- Dress modestly to avoid unwanted attention
- Avoid isolated areas, especially at night
- Use reputable transportation services
- Consider traveling with groups in some areas

## Restricted Areas and Special Permits

### What are the restricted and protected areas in India?

Some areas require special permits beyond the eVisa:

**Restricted Areas:**
- **Northeast India**: Parts of Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Mizoram, Manipur
- **Border Areas**: Certain regions near international borders
- **Islands**: Andaman & Nicobar Islands, Lakshadweep (some areas)
- **Ladakh**: Some areas require Inner Line Permits
- **Sikkim**: Some areas require permits

**Permit Requirements:**
- Check with local authorities or travel agents
- Apply for permits in advance
- Some permits available on arrival
- Different rules for different nationalities

**Important**: Always verify permit requirements for your specific destinations before traveling. Traveling to restricted areas without permits can result in serious consequences.

## Practical Tips for India Travel

### What should I pack for India?

**Essential Items:**
- Lightweight, modest clothing (cover shoulders and knees)
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
- Insect repellent
- Basic first-aid kit
- Prescription medications
- Copies of important documents
- Travel adapter for electronics

**Seasonal Considerations:**
- **Summer**: Light, breathable clothing, sun protection
- **Monsoon**: Rain gear, waterproof bags
- **Winter**: Warmer clothing for northern regions
- **Year-round**: Modest dress for religious sites

### What are the best practices for communication in India?

**Language:**
- English is widely spoken in tourist areas
- Learning basic Hindi phrases appreciated
- Use translation apps for local languages
- Be patient with language barriers

**Internet and Communication:**
- **SIM Cards**: Available at airports and shops (requires documentation)
- **WiFi**: Available in hotels, cafes, some public areas
- **Mobile Coverage**: Generally good in cities, variable in rural areas
- **Communication Apps**: WhatsApp widely used

### What are tipping customs in India?

**Tipping Guidelines:**
- **Restaurants**: 10% service charge often included, additional tip appreciated
- **Hotels**: 50-100 INR for porters, housekeeping
- **Taxis**: Round up fare or 10%
- **Tour Guides**: 200-500 INR per day
- **Drivers**: 200-500 INR per day

**Note**: Tipping is appreciated but not always mandatory. Use your discretion based on service quality.
