# New Zealand eVisa FAQ: Complete Guide to All Your Questions

Get comprehensive answers to all your New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) questions. This detailed FAQ covers requirements, application process, processing times, fees, travel tips, and everything you need to know about visiting New Zealand.

## General New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) Questions

### What is a New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA)?

A New Zealand Electronic Travel Authority (NZeTA) is an electronic travel authorization that allows eligible foreign nationals to enter New Zealand for tourism, business, or transit purposes. The NZeTA system enables travelers to apply online without visiting an embassy or consulate, making travel to New Zealand more convenient and accessible.

The NZeTA is linked electronically to your passport and allows multiple entries to New Zealand during its validity period. It's required for citizens of visa waiver countries and transit passengers.

### Who needs a New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA)?

The NZeTA is required for:

- **Citizens of visa waiver countries** visiting New Zealand
- **Transit passengers** (including those not leaving the international transit area)
- **Cruise ship passengers** arriving in New Zealand
- **Australian citizens** (who also need to pay the International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy)

**Visa Waiver Countries Include:**
- United States, Canada, United Kingdom
- Most European Union countries
- Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia
- Many other countries with visa waiver agreements

**Important**: Always verify your specific nationality's requirements using our eligibility checker before applying.

### How long is a New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) valid?

The NZeTA is valid for **2 years** from the date of issue or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. During this period, it allows:

- **Multiple entries** to New Zealand
- **Up to 3 months (90 days)** per visit for tourism or business purposes
- **Transit through New Zealand** airports

### What are the different types of New Zealand eVisas?

New Zealand offers the NZeTA for visa waiver countries:

**NZeTA (Electronic Travel Authority)**
- Purpose: Tourism, business meetings, transit
- Validity: 2 years from issue or until passport expiry
- Stay Duration: Up to 90 days per visit
- Entries: Multiple entries allowed
- Government Fee: NZ$12 (mobile app) or NZ$23 (online)
- IVL Fee: NZ$35 (International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy)
- Processing Time: 10 minutes to 72 hours

**Note**: For longer stays, work, or study, you'll need to apply for a traditional visa through New Zealand Immigration.

## Application Process Questions

### How do I apply for a New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA)?

Applying for a NZeTA is straightforward:

**Option 1: Mobile App (Faster and Cheaper)**
- Download the "NZeTA" mobile app
- Complete the application form
- Upload required documents
- Pay NZ$12 + NZ$35 IVL = NZ$47 total
- Processing: Usually 10 minutes to 72 hours

**Option 2: Online Website**
- Visit the official Immigration New Zealand website
- Complete the online application form
- Upload required documents
- Pay NZ$23 + NZ$35 IVL = NZ$58 total
- Processing: Usually within 72 hours

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. Choose your application method (app or website)
2. Complete the application form with accurate information
3. Upload passport photo and required documents
4. Pay the NZeTA fee and IVL
5. Receive confirmation and approval via email
6. Print or save your NZeTA confirmation for travel

**Additional Information Required:**

### What is the International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL)?

The IVL is a NZ$35 fee that:

- **Funds Conservation**: Supports conservation projects in New Zealand
- **Tourism Infrastructure**: Helps maintain and improve tourism facilities
- **Required for Most Visitors**: Applies to most travelers, including transit passengers
- **Valid for 2 Years**: Covers multiple visits during NZeTA validity

**IVL Exemptions:**
- Children under 2 years old
- New Zealand and Australian citizens and residents
- Transit passengers (under 24 hours, certain conditions)
- Diplomatic passport holders

### How long can I stay in New Zealand with an NZeTA?

The NZeTA allows you to stay in New Zealand for up to **3 months (90 days)** per visit for:

- Tourism and sightseeing
- Business meetings and conferences
- Visiting friends and family
- Short-term study (up to 3 months)

**Important**: The immigration officer at the border will determine the exact length of stay (up to 90 days).

### Can I extend my stay in New Zealand beyond 90 days?

With an NZeTA, you **cannot extend** your stay beyond 90 days. To stay longer, you must:

- Leave New Zealand before your 90 days expire
- Apply for a visitor visa for longer stays
- Return later (subject to immigration officer discretion)

**Planning Longer Stays:**
- Apply for a visitor visa before travel
- Consider multiple visits within the 2-year NZeTA validity
- Check visa requirements for extended stays

### Can I use my NZeTA for multiple entries?

Yes, the NZeTA allows **multiple entries** to New Zealand during its 2-year validity period. Each visit can be up to 90 days.

**Multiple Entry Benefits:**
- Visit New Zealand multiple times
- No need to reapply for 2 years
- Convenient for business travelers
- Flexible travel planning

### What happens at the border when I arrive in New Zealand?

Upon arrival in New Zealand with your NZeTA:

1. Present your valid passport (NZeTA is linked electronically)
2. Show your NZeTA confirmation (printed or digital)
3. Answer immigration officer's questions if asked
4. Receive entry stamp in your passport (if required)
5. Clear customs and biosecurity

The immigration officer will determine the exact length of stay (up to 90 days).

## Requirements and Eligibility Questions

### Do children need separate New Zealand eVisas (NZeTA)?

Yes, each traveler regardless of age needs their own NZeTA. This includes:

- Infants and children
- Minors traveling with parents
- Family members traveling together

**Important**: Even babies need their own NZeTA application.

### Can I apply for a New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) if I have a criminal record?

Having a criminal record doesn't automatically disqualify you from obtaining an NZeTA. However:

- You must declare any criminal history honestly in your application
- Each case is assessed individually by New Zealand immigration authorities
- Serious offenses may affect eligibility
- Some convictions may require a visitor visa instead

**Important**: Always be honest in your application. Providing false information can result in entry denial and future visa bans.

### What are the passport requirements for New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA)?

Your passport must meet these requirements:

- **Validity**: At least 3 months remaining beyond your intended stay
- **Nationality**: Must be from a visa waiver country
- **Condition**: Not damaged, torn, or defaced
- **Bio-data Page**: Clear and readable for scanning

If your passport doesn't meet these requirements, renew it before applying for your NZeTA.

### What if my passport expires while my NZeTA is still valid?

If you get a new passport, you **must apply for a new NZeTA** because:

- The NZeTA is linked to your specific passport number
- New passport = new NZeTA required
- Old NZeTA cannot be transferred to new passport

**Important**: Always ensure your passport is valid for at least 3 months beyond your intended stay.

## Processing and Status Questions

### How do I track my New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) application status?

You can track your application status through:

1. **Mobile App**: Check status in the NZeTA app
2. **Online Portal**: Use your application reference number
3. **Email Updates**: Check your email for confirmation
4. **Immigration New Zealand**: Contact directly if needed

**Application Status Options:**
- Application received
- Under review
- Approved
- Declined (with reason)

### What happens if my New Zealand eVisa (NZeTA) is declined?

If your NZeTA is declined:

1. You'll receive notification with the reason for decline
2. You may need to apply for a visitor visa instead
3. Common reasons include criminal history, health concerns, or previous immigration violations

**Common Reasons for Decline:**
- Previous immigration violations
- Criminal history
- Health concerns
- Insufficient information provided
- Security concerns

**Next Steps:**
- Review the decline reason
- Apply for a visitor visa if eligible
- Address any issues before reapplying
- Contact Immigration New Zealand for guidance

### Can I apply for NZeTA at the airport?

**No**, you **cannot** apply for NZeTA at the airport. You must:

- Apply online before traveling to New Zealand
- Airlines will check your NZeTA status before allowing you to board
- Without NZeTA, you may be denied boarding

**Important**: Always apply for your NZeTA well in advance of travel.

## Special Circumstances Questions

### Can I work in New Zealand with an NZeTA?

**No**, the NZeTA does **not** allow you to work in New Zealand. It's only for:

- Tourism and sightseeing
- Business meetings and conferences
- Visiting friends and family
- Short-term study (up to 3 months)

**To Work in New Zealand:**
- Apply for a work visa
- Meet work visa requirements
- Have a job offer or skills in demand

### Can I study in New Zealand with an NZeTA?

You can attend **short-term courses** (up to 3 months) with an NZeTA. For longer study programs, you need:

- A student visa
- Acceptance from a New Zealand educational institution
- Proof of financial support
- Health and character requirements

### Do I need NZeTA for connecting flights through New Zealand?

**Yes**, even if you're just transiting through New Zealand's international airport, you need:

- NZeTA application
- International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL) payment
- Valid passport

**Transit Requirements:**
- Apply for NZeTA before travel
- Pay IVL even for short transits
- Airlines will check NZeTA status before boarding

### What if I need to travel urgently and my NZeTA is still processing?

NZeTA processing cannot be expedited. If you need urgent travel:

- **Wait for Processing**: Usually 10 minutes to 72 hours
- **Apply Early**: Use mobile app for faster processing
- **Alternative**: Apply for a visitor visa with emergency processing (if eligible)

**Best Practice**: Always apply for your NZeTA at least 1 week before travel.

## Biosecurity and Customs Questions

### What are New Zealand's biosecurity requirements?

New Zealand has **strict biosecurity laws**. You must declare:

- **Food Items**: Any food, including snacks
- **Plant or Animal Products**: Seeds, plants, animal products
- **Outdoor Equipment**: Hiking boots, camping gear (may carry soil or seeds)
- **Medications**: Prescription medications with original labels
- **Wooden Items**: Carvings, souvenirs made from wood

**Important**: Failing to declare items can result in heavy fines. When in doubt, declare it.

### What items are prohibited from bringing into New Zealand?

**Prohibited Items:**
- Fresh fruits and vegetables
- Meat and meat products
- Dairy products
- Seeds and plants
- Firearms and weapons (without permits)
- Illegal drugs

**Restricted Items (Require Permits):**
- Medications (with prescriptions)
- Firearms (with permits)
- Certain food items
- Plants and seeds (with permits)
