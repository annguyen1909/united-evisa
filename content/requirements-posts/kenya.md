# Kenya Visa Requirements - Complete Guide 2024

Kenya, located in East Africa, is renowned for its diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. From the iconic Maasai Mara National Reserve to the pristine beaches of Mombasa, Kenya offers unforgettable experiences for every type of traveler.

## About Kenya

Kenya is home to the Great Rift Valley, Mount Kenya, and the Indian Ocean coastline, making it a perfect destination for safari adventures, mountain climbing, and beach holidays. Nairobi, the capital city, serves as a major hub for business and tourism in East Africa.

Kenya's visa system has been modernized with the introduction of the eVisa program, making it easier for international travelers to obtain their travel authorization online before arrival.

## Visa Requirements

### Required Documents for All Visa Types
- Valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay
- Recent passport-size photograph (taken within the last 6 months)
- Proof of accommodation in Kenya (hotel bookings or invitation letter)
- Return flight tickets or onward travel itinerary
- Proof of sufficient funds for the duration of stay
- Yellow fever vaccination certificate (if traveling from endemic areas)

### Additional Requirements for Business Visa
- Invitation letter from Kenyan business partner or organization
- Business registration documents (if applicable)
- Letter from employer stating purpose of visit
- Conference registration (if attending business events)
- Proof of business activities or meetings scheduled

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need a visa to visit Kenya?
Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter Kenya. However, citizens of East African Community (EAC) countries (Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, and South Sudan) do not need a visa. The Kenya eVisa system allows travelers from eligible countries to apply online before their trip.

### How long does it take to process a Kenya eVisa?
Processing time for Kenya eVisas ranges from 1-3 working days. We recommend applying at least 1 week before your intended travel date to avoid any delays.

### What documents do I need for a Kenya eVisa?
You'll need a valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your intended stay, a recent passport-size photo, proof of accommodation in Kenya, return flight tickets, and sufficient funds for your stay. Business travelers may need additional documents like invitation letters.

### Can I extend my Kenya visa?
Yes, you can extend your Kenya visa while in the country. You'll need to visit the Immigration Department in Nairobi or other major cities. Extensions are typically granted for valid reasons such as medical treatment, business needs, or tourism. There are fees associated with visa extensions.

### Is it safe to travel to Kenya?
Kenya is generally safe for tourists, especially in popular tourist areas like Nairobi, Mombasa, and the Maasai Mara. However, it's important to stay informed about current travel advisories, avoid certain areas near the border with Somalia, and take standard safety precautions like not walking alone at night.

### What is the best time to visit Kenya?
The best time to visit Kenya depends on what you want to see. For wildlife viewing, the dry seasons (January-March and July-October) are ideal. The wildebeest migration typically occurs from July to October. For beach holidays, the coastal areas are pleasant year-round, though April-May and November are the rainy seasons.
